Free programs for youth aging out of foster care — the 2026 directory

The short answer: there is real money and real help out there, and almost none of it costs you anything. Here are the programs that actually deliver — education money up to $5,000/year, free job training with housing, tuition waivers, health insurance to 26, and business ownership training — with who qualifies and how to start. We list programs run by other organizations honestly, because the point is to get you what you need.

By Azgari Foundation · Updated July 9, 2026 · 8 min read

Money for school & training

Chafee Education & Training Voucher (ETV)

Ages up to 26 · were in care after 16th birthday

Up to $5,000 per academic year for college or career training — and it covers rent, books, a computer, childcare, and transportation, not just tuition. Stacks on top of Pell Grants and FAFSA aid. Apply through your state's ETV or independent-living office (search "ETV" + your state).

State tuition & fee waivers

Former foster youth · public colleges

Many states waive tuition entirely at public colleges for former foster youth. In Texas, if you were in DFPS conservatorship (e.g., the day before your 18th birthday, or at 14 if eligible for adoption), tuition and fees are waived at state colleges — and you do not have to be in the PAL program to qualify.

Independent FAFSA status

In foster care any time after age 13

You count as an independent student, so you don't report a parent's or foster parent's income — which usually unlocks far more aid. Get help from someone who knows foster-youth FAFSA rules so the foster-care questions are answered correctly.

Free job training (some with housing)

Job Corps

Ages 16–24 · free · includes housing & meals

Free career training in trades, healthcare, IT, and more — plus housing, meals, basic health care, and a small living allowance at residential centers nationwide. Foster youth automatically qualify as low-income. The single best option if you need training and a place to live at the same time.

WIOA Youth Program

Ages 16–24 · free · in every state

Free job training, paid work experience, tutoring, mentoring, financial literacy, and even entrepreneurship training. Foster youth and youth who aged out are explicitly eligible. Find your local provider through your state workforce board or by dialing 211.

YouthBuild

Ages 16–24 · left school without a diploma

Earn your diploma while learning construction and other in-demand trades — and you build affordable housing in your own community. Leads into paid apprenticeships (earn while you learn).

Registered apprenticeships

Ages 16–24 · get paid from day one

You earn a paycheck while you train, with classroom instruction built in. No student debt. Search apprenticeship.gov for openings near you.

Health, housing & basics

Medicaid until 26

Were in foster care at 18 · any state · any income

Free health insurance until your 26th birthday under federal law — the same coverage other young adults get on a parent's plan. You may need to enroll; it isn't automatic in every state, especially if you move.

Extended foster care & FYI housing vouchers

Under 21

Extended foster care (to 21) can pay toward your own apartment. HUD's Foster Youth to Independence (FYI) vouchers provide rental help specifically for youth leaving care — ask your caseworker or local housing authority.

211 & findhelp.org

Everyone · free · 24/7

Dial 211 (or 877-541-7905 in Texas) any time to reach real local help — food, housing, counseling, transportation. findhelp.org lets you search the same resources online by zip code.

Something you own

Azgari Foundation — AI Agency Business Owner Training

Ages 16–25 · tuition-free · fully online

Most programs point you toward a job. This one helps you build a business of your own — an AI-powered marketing business — in six months, with nine volunteer educators and one-to-one mentorship. Free means free. Published by the Foundation behind this guide.
