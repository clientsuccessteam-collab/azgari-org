How to make money at 18 with no degree and no family safety net

The short answer: stack two things. Get a job that hires now for cash this week, and start free training or a business on the side that pays more later. There's no honest shortcut — anyone promising fast riches is selling something. But there are real paths that take you from surviving to owning, and none of them require a degree or money to start. Here they are, ranked.

By Azgari Foundation · Updated July 9, 2026 · 7 min read
Ranked by: how fast it pays × how far it takes you

1. Get cash coming in this week

You need money now, and that's okay — a job today buys you the runway to build something bigger. Roles that hire 18-year-olds with no degree or experience, often $15–20+/hour:

  • Warehouse & fulfillment (Amazon, UPS, local distribution)
  • Delivery & rideshare (if you have a car)
  • Food service & retail — many train you on the spot
  • Caregiving / home health aide — steady demand, some pay for your certification
  • Landscaping, moving, warehouse temp agencies — start within days
This is the floor, not the ceiling. A job stabilizes you. What you do with your off hours is what changes your income in a year.

2. Free training that pays you while you learn

Bigger money · a few months to a year

You can get trained for skilled work without paying tuition or taking on debt — and some of these pay you during training:

  • Registered apprenticeships — earn a paycheck from day one while you learn a trade (electrical, plumbing, IT, healthcare). Search apprenticeship.gov.
  • WIOA Youth programs — free training plus paid work experience, in every state. Foster youth qualify automatically.
  • Job Corps — free training that also gives you housing, meals, and a living allowance if you need a place to stay.

See the full breakdown in our directory of free programs.

3. Build something you own

Highest ceiling · biggest effort

A job pays you for your time. A business pays you for the value you create — and no one can lay you off from something you own. The lowest-cost businesses to start are service businesses: you sell a skill, not inventory. Cleaning, tutoring, lawn care, social media management, and AI-powered marketing services all start with effort instead of money.

AI tools have quietly made this reachable. A 19-year-old with a laptop can now deliver marketing work that used to require a team. The hard part isn't the tools — it's knowing what to sell, how to price it, how to find a first customer, and how to keep going after a "no." That's exactly what coaching is for.

Real talk: a business is slower to pay than a job, and not every business works. That's why the smart move is to keep a job for cash while you build. Anyone who tells you to quit everything and "go all in" with no income is giving you bad advice.
