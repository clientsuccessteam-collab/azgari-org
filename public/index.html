Why this exists
20,000

Young adults age out of the American foster care system every year — and the system stops seeing them the day the case closes.

1 in 5
Becomes homeless the day they age out — instantly, with nowhere to go.
— National Foster Youth Institute
1 in 4
Will be involved with the justice system within two years of leaving care.
— National Foster Youth Institute
~3%
Will earn a four-year college degree by their mid-twenties.
— Chapin Hall, Midwest Study

These young people are not short on talent or determination. What they're missing is what most business owners started with: one steady adult in their corner, and a fair chance to build something of their own. This program exists to provide both.

The program

Not a certificate. A working business.

Six months. Fully online. Free to attend. Every participant builds their own AI-powered marketing business — the tools, the training, and the coaching to go after their first paying customer before the program ends. A business with their name on it.

01
Aug — Oct 2026
Train + Build

The first three months build the foundations. Participants learn to think like business owners — money, customers, and the tools of the trade — before they ever pitch one.

  • Foundations of business ownership and small-business thinking
  • Financial literacy and personal budgeting
  • AI tools, prompt engineering, and workflow automation
  • Service design, packaging, and pricing
  • Brand identity and marketing fundamentals
  • Agency infrastructure: CRM, contracts, invoicing, back office
OutcomeA named business, set up properly, with a real service to offer.
02
Nov — Dec 2026
First Clients

Months four and five are about real customers. Participants reach out, quote real work, and deliver it — with a mentor beside them for every step.

  • Sales conversations, outreach — and how to handle a no
  • Finding potential customers and staying organized
  • Delivering AI-powered marketing services to real clients
  • Client onboarding, communication, and follow-through
  • One-to-one mentorship on every client project
  • Tracking the money and building repeat business
GoalA first paying customer — earned, not given.
03
Jan 2027
Close + Launch

The final month closes the cohort and opens the year ahead — because a business is a beginning, not a finish line.

  • Growing carefully: when to get help and when to add services
  • Long-term financial planning and tax preparedness
  • A professional peer network that outlasts the cohort
  • Public graduation and launch celebration
  • Six months of continued mentorship post-graduation
  • Pathways into further certifications and resources
OutcomeA graduate who owns a business — and people who keep showing up.

Concept rendering · What we're building toward

The campus we're building.

A wellness-focused live-and-learn campus where opportunity youth spend up to a year building a business, a body, and a future.

The pilot cohort is where we start. But the vision is bigger than six months of online classes. It's a place. It's a year. It's the difference between graduating with a certificate and graduating with a future you own outright.

A place where a young person can arrive without an adult in their corner — and leave with a business, a network, and a plan.

Cottages to live in. A learning hall. Hydroponic towers and a working greenhouse. A kitchen where they learn to cook the food they grow. Space to lift, to run, to breathe. A pond, a garden — and most of all, a network of adults who show up for them until they no longer need the campus at all.

This is what we're raising for. This is where we're going.

01

A Home

Stable, dignified housing on campus for up to a year while participants learn and build.

A Livelihood

Their own micro-business or high-demand job skills — an income they own outright.

A Healthy Life

Nutrition, fitness, mental health, and financial literacy woven into every day.

Where the money goes. What we deliver.

Azgari Foundation is a Delaware 501(c)(3) public charity — IRC §170(b)(1)(A)(vi) — with an active SAM.gov registration and an IRS determination effective December 1, 2025. We are structured to receive workforce agency contracts, federal and state grants, foundation awards, and individual philanthropy. Programs are free to participants and always will be.

Since January 2026 the Foundation has delivered scholarship-funded entrepreneurship education while designing this pilot — eight scholarships awarded to date; two participants operate active businesses today as a direct result of the program, and three more are employed while building toward ownership.

Populations we serve

Opportunity youth ages 16–25, with a focus on foster care alumni, current and former Preparation for Adult Living (PAL) participants, young adults exiting the juvenile justice system, Community-Based Care (CBC) referrals, and unaccompanied youth facing housing instability — priority populations under WIOA Title I Youth Formula, Chafee Foster Care Independence, and Youth Homelessness Demonstration Program (YHDP) funding streams.

Outcomes we track

  • Credential attainment — certificate in AI-powered agency operations
  • Employment or self-employment at exit, 6- and 12-month follow-up
  • Revenue generated by participant-owned businesses
  • Housing stability at exit and follow-up
  • Median earnings twelve months post-program

Funding vehicles we accept

  • WIOA Youth Formula contracted services (in- and out-of-school youth)
  • Chafee Foster Care Independence Program grants
  • HUD Youth Homelessness Demonstration Program and CoC awards
  • Texas Workforce Commission (TWC) workforce and apprenticeship contracts
  • SBA PRIME and community capital-access programs
  • Foundation program-related and mission-related investments
  • Corporate philanthropy, employee giving, and graduate sponsorship

Regional partners we're engaging

Opportunity Austin, Austin Community Foundation, Workforce Solutions Capital Area, Texas Workforce Commission Foster Youth Task Force and Region 7 PAL leadership, Texas Homeless Network Balance of State Continuum of Care, Texas DFPS, LifeWorks Austin, Capital A Housing, PeopleFund, and the Federal Home Loan Bank of Dallas Affordable Housing Program.
