# 04 — Tides Center / Tides Foundation — BACKUP

**Website:** https://tides.org
**Application page:** https://tides.org/services/fiscal-sponsorship (verify current URL)
**Fee:** 9%+ (verify — may include additional service fees)
**Typical approval time:** 6–8 weeks
**Why they're on the list:** Tides is the largest and best-known fiscal sponsor in the U.S. Grants staff at major foundations recognize the name immediately, which can help with certain national funders. However, Tides is the most selective and slowest of the four — treat as backup and start the inquiry in parallel so it's in motion if faster sponsors don't work out.

**Honest note:** Tides historically focuses on social-justice, progressive-movement, and larger-scale projects. Smaller, newer organizations sometimes don't get accepted. Apply anyway — it costs nothing to try, and if they accept, you have the strongest national brand backing you.

---

## How to submit

Tides requires an initial inquiry before a formal application. Start with their online contact form or inquiry email.

**Flow:**
1. Submit inquiry on tides.org.
2. If they invite further discussion, a phone or video call with their fiscal-sponsorship team.
3. If invited, submit formal application packet.
4. Internal review cycle.

## Tips specific to Tides

- Tides is selective about mission fit. Lean into the **economic-mobility** and **underserved-communities** framing (matches their priorities) more than **entrepreneurship education** alone.
- Tides asks for a **thorough governance narrative** — reference https://azgari.org/transparency directly.
- Expect **multiple rounds of questions** before acceptance. This is normal for Tides.
- Tides can take **6–8 weeks to decide**. Do not wait for them — accept PPF or Social Good Fund first if they come back faster.

## After submission

- Acknowledgment: 5–10 business days.
- Intake conversation (if invited): 2–4 weeks after inquiry.
- Formal review cycle: 4–8 weeks.
- If Tides declines, that's common and not a reflection on the Foundation — they have a narrower acceptance rate than our top two choices.
