## Why this packet exists

Your 501(c)(3) determination is pending. A fiscal sponsor is an existing 501(c)(3) that hosts your project while you wait — they can accept grants and tax-deductible donations on your behalf and pass the funds to you, usually for a fee of 4–11% of what they raise.

Signing with a fiscal sponsor unlocks almost every foundation, corporate community grant, DAF gift, and employee-matching program the Foundation needs in Year 1. **Do this first. Everything else waits.**

## The four sponsors to apply to (in priority order)

| # | Sponsor | Fee | Typical approval time | Why we chose it |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Players Philanthropy Fund (PPF)** | 4% | 5–10 business days | Lowest fee in the industry; fast approval; no geographic restriction. First choice. |
| 2 | **Social Good Fund** | 7% | 2–4 weeks | Widely accepted by foundations; strong infrastructure; accepts first-time founders. |
| 3 | **Community Partners** (LA-based, national) | 9–11% | 4–6 weeks | Best for organizations planning multi-year operations and capital campaigns. More selective but strong brand. |
| 4 | **Tides Center** | 9%+ | 6–8 weeks | Strongest national brand recognition with major foundations; most rigorous review, so save as backup. |

Apply to **all four in parallel.** Whichever approves first and feels right operationally, sign with. You can turn down the others. Double-fiscal-sponsorship is not standard — you pick one.

## Required attachments (gather these before you start)

These are the documents every fiscal sponsor will ask for. Put them in a single Google Drive folder so you can attach them fast:

- [ ] **Certificate of Incorporation** — Delaware Secretary of State filing (the PDF you received when the Foundation was incorporated in January 2026).
- [ ] **Foreign-entity registration confirmations** — Texas and Florida Secretary of State filings.
- [ ] **IRS Form 1023 filing confirmation** — the acknowledgment letter or submission-receipt PDF.
- [ ] **EIN confirmation (IRS Form CP-575 or SS-4 notice)** — the document with EIN `41-2896092`.
- [ ] **Bylaws** — if adopted; otherwise note "in process."
- [ ] **Board of Directors roster** — even if provisional; sponsors want to see governance exists. If there is no independent board yet, note "board seating in progress" and list the founder plus any advisors.
- [ ] **Articles of Incorporation** — often the same as the Certificate of Incorporation depending on the state.
- [ ] **Conflict-of-Interest Policy** — standard IRS-suggested template is fine if you've adopted one.
- [ ] **Founder's bio / CV** — one page about Azgari Lipshy.
- [ ] **Budget for 2026** — simple one-page projected budget. (Draft below in `00-master-application-answers.md` under "Budget Summary.")
- [ ] **W-9 for Azgari Foundation** — sponsors need this even though they'll primarily use their own EIN during the sponsorship.
- [ ] **Driver's license or government ID for the authorized signatory (Azgari Lipshy)** — most sponsors now require identity verification.

If any of these don't exist yet, the sponsor will usually accept a good-faith note — but it slows approval. Have as many as possible ready.

## Honest caveats

- **Fee structures change.** The fee percentages above are current as of this packet's writing but verify on each sponsor's site before you assume. Some sponsors have recently added flat annual minimums.
- **Approval rates vary.** PPF and Social Good Fund approve most qualifying projects. Community Partners and Tides are more selective; a "no" from them isn't a red flag — it just means they have a different strategic focus.
- **Not legal advice.** A fiscal-sponsorship agreement is a binding contract. Have a nonprofit attorney review the agreement before signing if you haven't before — most sponsors' standard templates are fine, but know what you're signing.
