## SECTION 2 — Mission, Activities, and Charitable Class

**Mission statement (short form):**
Entrepreneurship education, workforce development, and economic mobility for out-of-school youth, aging-out foster youth, justice-involved young adults, and low-income veterans.

**Mission statement (full form):**
Azgari Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit entrepreneurship-education and workforce-development organization serving out-of-school youth, aging-out foster youth, justice-involved young adults, and low-income veterans. Our current program delivers a 24-week online entrepreneurship-education curriculum with scholarship support. As funding permits, the Foundation is developing transitional-housing programs that pair residential stability with basic-needs support, training stipends, and post-program job-placement and micro-enterprise seed grants — to expand economic mobility and create jobs in underserved communities.

**What does the Foundation do today (currently operating programs)?**
The Foundation operates a 24-week online entrepreneurship-education curriculum delivered at no cost to participants through scholarships. The curriculum covers business-model selection, compliance, branding, sales methodology, operations, and funding readiness. Mentor-supported practicum is embedded throughout. As of April 2026, eight scholarship participants have been enrolled since the Foundation launched in January 2026. Outcome data is collected continuously and will be published as cohorts complete the program.

**What does the Foundation plan to do (not yet operating)?**
Contingent on government grants, foundation awards, and capital-campaign funding, the Foundation plans to add: (1) transitional housing for residential participants; (2) basic-needs and training stipends during the 24-week curriculum; (3) post-program job-placement support and micro-enterprise seed grants structured as an approved workforce outcome; and (4) a longer-term self-sustaining campus with vertical farming and renewable-energy infrastructure. None of these programs are operating today.

**Charitable class served:**
The Foundation directs its scholarships and planned programming to young adults facing real barriers to economic mobility, specifically:

1. **Out-of-school youth** — ages 16–24 who are not in school and are working toward economic self-sufficiency.
2. **Aging-out foster youth** — ages 18–24 transitioning out of the foster care system.
3. **Justice-involved young adults** — ages 18–24 reentering their communities after involvement with the justice system.
4. **Low-income veterans** — military members transitioning to civilian careers, including those facing housing instability.

**Geographic service area:**
Nationwide via the online curriculum, with a primary focus on Texas and Florida. Delaware incorporation; Texas and Florida foreign-entity registrations.

**IRS Subsection and Public Charity classification (planned):**
501(c)(3) public charity. Expected classification under IRC § 509(a)(1) / 170(b)(1)(A)(vi) (publicly supported) or § 509(a)(2) (gross-receipts supported), depending on the revenue mix during the first two fiscal years. Our Form 1023 has been filed and is awaiting IRS determination.

## SECTION 6 — Project Narrative (if required)

Many fiscal sponsors ask for a 500–1,000-word narrative. Paste this:

---

Azgari Foundation is a 501(c)(3)-applicant nonprofit incorporated in Delaware in January 2026, with foreign-entity registrations in Texas and Florida. Our mission is to increase economic mobility, expand access to business ownership, and create jobs in underserved communities through entrepreneurship education, mentorship, and workforce development programs.

**The problem we address.** Out-of-school youth aged 16–24, aging-out foster youth transitioning from the foster care system, justice-involved young adults reentering their communities, and low-income veterans face structural barriers to stable employment and business ownership. These populations are over-represented in long-term poverty, housing instability, and cycles of disadvantage — and they are under-represented in business ownership, the most durable path to intergenerational wealth.

**Our response.** The Foundation operates a 24-week online entrepreneurship-education curriculum delivered at no cost through scholarships. The curriculum covers business-model selection, compliance, branding, sales methodology, operations, and funding readiness, with mentor-supported practicum throughout. Participants do not pay tuition; the program exists to open entrepreneurship pathways to populations historically excluded from them.

**Where we are today.** The Foundation launched in January 2026. As of April 2026, we have awarded scholarships to eight participants across our first cohorts. We are measuring outcomes continuously: program completion rate, credential attainment, post-program employment and self-employment status, and median wage gains. Outcome data will be published in our first Annual Report.

**Where we're going.** Contingent on grant capital, the Foundation plans to add (1) transitional housing paired with the curriculum, so participants without stable housing can access the program; (2) basic-needs and training stipends during the 24-week curriculum; (3) post-program job-placement support and micro-enterprise seed grants structured as an approved workforce outcome in alignment with federal workforce-development programs; and (4) a longer-term self-sustaining campus. None of these are currently operating.

**Why a fiscal sponsor now.** Our IRS Form 1023 determination is pending. Almost every foundation, corporate community-grant program, and donor-advised-fund holder that would consider supporting the Foundation during Year 1 requires a 501(c)(3)-recognized recipient. A fiscal sponsor bridges this gap, allowing us to accept tax-deductible contributions, apply for foundation grants, and begin building a track record of program outcomes during the determination-pending period.

**Who we are not.** The Foundation does not sell fee-based programs on azgari.org. Any fee-based educational offerings are operated entirely separately by the for-profit sister brand at azgari.com. The Foundation does not guarantee any specific level of income or business success for participants; outcomes vary based on effort, market conditions, and capital. The Foundation does not engage in political-campaign activity, consistent with IRC § 501(c)(3) restrictions.

**The leadership.** Azgari Foundation was founded by Azgari Lipshy, a serial entrepreneur with over 20 years of experience operating service businesses. The Foundation's board governance, conflict-of-interest policies, and financial-reporting commitments are published on the Foundation's Governance & Transparency page at https://azgari.org/transparency.

Thank you for considering this application.
