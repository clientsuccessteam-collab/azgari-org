# 03 — Community Partners

**Website:** https://communitypartners.org
**Application page:** https://communitypartners.org/fiscal-sponsorship (verify current URL)
**Fee:** 9–11% of funds raised (verify; tiered based on revenue)
**Typical approval time:** 4–6 weeks
**Why they're on the list:** Strongest infrastructure for organizations planning multi-year operations, capital campaigns, and larger federal grants. Worth the higher fee if you anticipate multi-year grant cycles or need a more institutional-looking sponsor for certain foundation applications.

**Note:** Community Partners is headquartered in Los Angeles but operates nationally. They are more selective than PPF and Social Good Fund — apply, but treat PPF/SGF as more likely first approvals.

---

## How to submit

Community Partners has a multi-step intake:

1. **Initial inquiry form** on their site.
2. **Phone or video call** with their intake team (15–30 minutes).
3. **Formal application packet** with attachments.
4. **Board review** — they convene regularly to review new projects.

Start with step 1 (the inquiry form) this week.

## Tips specific to Community Partners

- Community Partners does a more thorough due-diligence process than PPF or SGF. Allow 4–6 weeks and don't be surprised by follow-up questions.
- They prefer a **written theory of change** and **logic model** — both are already public at https://azgari.org/funders. Link there directly.
- Community Partners tends to favor projects with clear **multi-year plans** and **larger budgets** ($100K+). The $250K Year-1 target fits.
- Community Partners can be valuable specifically for **California foundations** if the Foundation later targets CA funders — they have deep CA relationships.

## After submission

- Inquiry-form acknowledgment: 3–7 business days.
- Intake call scheduled: 1–2 weeks after inquiry.
- Formal application review: 2–4 weeks after application packet submission.
- Total expected timeline from inquiry to decision: 4–6 weeks.
